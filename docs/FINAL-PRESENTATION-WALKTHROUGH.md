# Final Presentation Walkthrough

## 1. Problem

Underground mine teams need safer ways to inspect tunnels before workers enter. Gas, oxygen, dust, signal loss, blocked routes, and poor visibility can change quickly.

## 2. Operation MOLE Solution

Operation MOLE is a single-drone inspection and decision-support prototype. It combines simulated drone telemetry, sensor readings, alerts, reports, safety approval, and role-based workflows.

## 3. Single-Drone Mission

Show MOLE-01 from the dashboard and drone health page. Explain that the prototype focuses on one rugged underground inspection drone, not a multi-drone relay fleet.

## 4. Live Telemetry

Open a mission from `/app/missions` with the Live link. Show connection status, map, current zone, travelled route, battery, signal, latest readings, active alerts, and mission event log.

## 5. Alert Handling

Run the persisted mission simulation from `/app/dashboard`. Open the generated high or critical alert, assign or update status, and explain how alert notifications appear.

## 6. Report Generation

Open `/app/reports` and a mission report. Show zones scanned, hazards detected, final entry decision, and print/PDF-friendly view.

## 7. Safety Approval

As Administrator or Safety Officer, submit a safety approval decision on a completed report. Explain that active missions cannot be approved until complete.

## 8. Business Model

Position the project as a B2B Drone-as-a-Service / Inspection-as-a-Service prototype for mines that need inspection records, audit trails, and safety decision support.

## 9. Thailand 4.0 Alignment

Connect the prototype to smart industry, worker safety, robotics, data platforms, and applied digital transformation for mining operations.

## 10. Australia-Thailand Collaboration

Frame the concept as a collaboration opportunity: Australian mine-safety practices and drone robotics combined with Thai industrial modernization and local mine operations.

## Required Disclaimer

Operation MOLE is a student prototype. Drone telemetry, sensor readings, hazard detection, and mission behavior are simulated and are not certified for real-world safety decisions.
